Let's cook some Carbonara!

Description

Carbonara is a classic Roman pasta dish that celebrates simplicity and indulgence. It's made with spaghetti or other long pasta varieties, mixed with a creamy sauce created from eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, black pepper, and guanciale (Italian cured pork cheek).

Ingredients

(4 servings)

  • 320 grams of spaghetti
  • 150 grams of guanciale
  • 50 grams of Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 6 egg yolks
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the guanciale
    Remove the pork rind from the guanciale and cut it first into slices and then into strips about 1cm thick. Put everything into a non-stick pan and let it brown for about 10 to 15 minutes, being careful not to burn it
  2. Cook the pasta
    While the guanciale cooks, get some water to boil in a pot. When it starts boiling add some salt (not too much, the meat and dish will provide loads of sodium on their own) and throw the pasta in; cook for the indicated time on the package.
  3. Stiring
    Drain the pasta and put it directly into the pan with the guanciale; Stir it briefly then remove the pan from the fire, add the egg yolks and Pecorino Romano cheese and mix everything up. If too dry, add some cooking water
  4. Enjoy
